 ~ Eating Habits ~

Wolves play a key role in keeping ecosystems healthy. They help keep deer and elk populations in an orderly population, which benefits other plants and animal species. The carcasses of their prey also help to redistribute nutrients and provide food for other wildlife species. Wolves eat ungulates, or large hoofed mammals; for example, elk, deer, moose and caribou, as well as beaver, rabbits and other small prey. Wolves are also scavengers and often eat animals that have died due to other causes.
